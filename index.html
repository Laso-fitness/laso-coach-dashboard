What separates Laso from a weight vest

            
A weight vest loads the spine vertically and compresses the joints. The Laso — a center of gravity loading system — distributes load at the body's natural center of mass using the Golden Ratio (1:1.618) bead sizing and 7-point magnetic closure. This means the skeleton, joints, and muscles engage in the same plane they were designed to work in. The stimulus is biomechanically aligned. The stress is productive, not compressive.

The Infinite
Repetition Theory

        
"A repetition is not a count. It is an infinite collection of positions in space — every point away from your center of gravity where the muscle is under tension. Any stimulus is part of the rep. The goal: keep the muscle working across as many of those positions as possible, for as long as possible."

        
— Reginald J. Gardiner II, Founder · Laso Fitness

The visibility principle

        
"To understand the muscle, we have to see it. In its active state. The muscle must spend as much time as possible in its stimulated position. That is the repetition."

        
— Reginald J. Gardiner II, Founder · Laso Fitness

        

          

            👁️
            
See the muscle contract
Watch for the belly of the muscle to visibly shorten and harden under load. That visual confirmation is the stimulus signal — the muscle is doing its job across this position in space.

          

          

            ⏱️
            
Slow down to collect more positions
A faster rep moves through more positions but collects less tension per position. Slowing the tempo is how you claim more of the infinite arc — turning 10 reps into the equivalent of 40.

          

          

            🔬
            
Future: camera tracking (App-E-Tight v2)
Coming in a future version: pose estimation via your phone camera. Real-time joint angle tracking, length-tension curve analysis, and a quantified stimulus score per rep — turning this philosophy into measurable data.

Guided meditation — Laso method

        

          

            

              
Pre-session activation

              3 min
            

            
Close your eyes. Scan your body from feet to crown. Identify where tension lives. Breathe into each area. Visualize the muscles you are about to train — see them contracting across their full arc. See each position they will occupy. Prime the CNS before the first rep.

          

          

            

              
The infinite arc visualization

              5 min
            

            
Sit comfortably. Breathe slowly. In your mind, hold a weight. Feel it extend away from your center. Notice that there are infinite positions between where you start and where you finish. Each one is real. Each one is work. You are not counting reps — you are collecting positions. Move deliberately. Collect as many as you can.

          

          

            

              
Post-session integration

              4 min
            

            
Lie down or sit still. Breathe out any remaining tension. Your muscles are now rebuilding. Your CNS is encoding what just happened. This stillness is not rest — it is active adaptation. Let your body integrate the stimulus. Feel the warmth in the muscles. That warmth is positive change happening in real time.

          

        

      


      
      

        
Where this fits in your program

        

          

            Before
            
Box breathing or power breath — prime the CNS, raise focus, set the intention for the session

          

          

            Between sets
            
3–4 slow diaphragmatic breaths — lower heart rate deliberately, maintain parasympathetic tone, stay in the zone

          

          

            After
            
4-7-8 or diaphragmatic reset — activate recovery, reduce cortisol, begin the adaptation window

          

        

        
These are not optional additions to the Laso program — they are structural components. The nervous system that trains is the same nervous system that breathes. Both need to be coached.
